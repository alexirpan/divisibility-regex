How does this work?

This constructs a discrete finite automaton (DFA) that accepts a string if and only if it is divisible by a given number. Then, that DFA is converted to a regular expression.

I used the Brzozowski Algebraic Method to do this, although several other approaches exist.

Why are these regular expressions so long?

The Brzozowski method is guaranteed to return an equivalent regular expression. It is not guaranteed to return a short one. In fact, finding the minimal regular expression that matches a language (set of strings) is PSPACE-Complete, and the minimal regular expression for a DFA with n states may be exponential in n.

That being said, I optimized for correctness instead of length, so there are lots of extra parantheses.

Why does this only check divisibility up to 9?

In principle, this algorithm works on any DFA, and can output regular expressions that check divisibility by larger numbers. However, the length grows exponentially in the number of states. Dividing by anything past around 15 takes too long.
